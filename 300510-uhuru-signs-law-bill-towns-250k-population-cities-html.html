Uhuru signs into law Bill that will see towns with 250K population become cities

Publish date: 2024-08-17

- The three Bills presented to the president were; Urban Areas and Cities (Amendment) Bill 2017, Petroleum Bill 2017 and the Energy Bill 2017.

- The Urban Areas and Cities Bill will see towns with 250k residents become cities

- The new law would also qualify urban centres with 50k residents to be municipalities.

- The Petroleum Bill will see county governments get 20% of the resources while the local community would receive 5% of the national share

President Uhuru Kenyatta on Tuesday, March 12 signed three bills into law after they were presented to him by the Senate.

Assenting into law the Urban Areas and Cities Amendment Bill, Uhuru said the new law would help accelerate the efficient and quality service delivery to Kenyans through the devolved units.

TUKO.co.ke understands that the new law would enable county governments review the criteria provided for classifying an area as a city, municipality, town or market centre.

The law also reviewed the number of people required for a town to qualify as a city by reducing the population from 500,000 to 250,000 people.

The same law will also see a county government declare an urban area a municipality if it attains a resident population of at least 50,000 people.

For an area to be declared a town, it would be required to attain a population of at least 10,000 residents while a market centre would only require a population of at least 2,000 residents.

County governments would also be required to establish boards to govern and manage cities and municipalities.

The new Energy law among other things proposed the establishment of three key national energy entities that would manage and regulate energy resources in the country.

It proposed formation of the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority which would be tasked to regulate generation, importation, exportation, transmission, distribution, supply and usage of electrical energy with the exception of licensing of nuclear facilities.

The 2017 Petroleum Bill was also assented into law by the President and would among other things provide a framework for contracting, exploring, developing and producing petroleum.

Under this law, the national government, county governments and local communities shall receive a fair share of benefit from revenues emanating from petroleum operations.

A county government will receive a share equivalent to 20% of the national government’s share while local communities will receive a share equivalent to 5% of the national government share.

Parliament would be reviewing the percentages within 10 years to factor in any adjustments needed.

Present during assenting of the three Bills were Speaker of the National Assembly Justin Muturi and his Senate counterpart Ken Lusaka, Majority leader in the National Assembly Aden Duale, his Senate counterpart Kipchumba Murkomen.

Also present were Senator James Orengo, Clerk to The National Assembly Michael Sialai and his senate counterpart Jeremiah Nyegenye among others.
