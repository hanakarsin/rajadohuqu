Tanzania Suspends Uhuru Newspaper for Alleging President Samia Suluhu Won't Vie in 2025

Publish date: 2024-03-29
  • The Tanzanian government has suspended the ruling party's publication Uhuru newspaper for 14 days
  • This is after the media outlet ran a story alleging that President Samia Suluhu has no intentions of running for presidency in 2025
  • The move comes three months after the incumbent lifted suspensions on other media houses that had been sanctioned by the former regime

The Tanzanian government has suspended a local daily for running a story that alleged President Samia Suluhu Hassan will not vie for office in 2025.

That makes Uhuru newspaper, which is owned by the ruling party Chama cha Mapinduzi (CCM), the first publication to be suspended during her tenure.

According to a report by The Citizen, the government maintains the lead story distorted excerpts from an interview President Samia held with British Broadcasting Corporation (BBC) in Dar es Salaam.

"Sina wazo kuwania urais 2025 - Samia,” (I have no intention of running for the presidency in 2025 - Samia") read the disputed headline.

Statement from Government

A statement signed by Shaka Hamdu Shaka, secretary of the CCM central committee, ideology and publicity maintained that the newspaper grossly misrepresented the president’s word.

"Strict disciplinary action will be taken against all those involved in its preparation," the statement reads in part.

It further urged members of the public to ignore the misleading information as it was spread by individuals who harbour ‘personal interests.’

Additionally, the statement clarified that the head of state is currently focused on serving Tanzanians and not the next election.

Suspension Lasts 14 Days

Reuters records that the management board of Uhuru newspaper has suspended three top officials among them the CEO over the misleading story.

Investigations are ongoing into what led to the mishap, and the newspaper will be allowed to file an appeal to the minister of information after 14 days.

The suspension comes three months after Samia's government ordered for the reopening of media houses that had been closed during her predecessor John Magufuli's tenure.

"I am told you revoked licenses of some media outlets, including some online television stations. You should lift the ban but tell them to follow the law and government guidelines," she said in April.

Her sentiments lifted the lid on what many had viewed as curtailing of press freedom by the former regime as media houses were shut down for rubbing Magufuli the wrong way.
