12 killed in freak road crash at Salgaa on Nakuru-Eldoret highway

Publish date: 2024-06-25

- At least 12 people lost their lives on Sunday, November 19 after a freak road crash at Salagaa on Nakuru-Eldoret highway

- The accident was caused after a Great Rift Shuttle hit a cow before the drive lost control and collided with a Lorry in the 3am incident

-Police confirmed the incident and urged motorists to be careful while on the road especially at night

Kenyans woke up to sad news on Sunday, November 19 following a freak road crash at Salgaa on the Nakuru-Eldoret highway that claimed the lives of 12 people.

All the 11 passengers and the driver of the ill-fated Great Rift Shuttle perished after the vehicle reportedly hit a cow at the black spot before the driver lost control and rammed into an oncoming truck.

According to reports by Daily Nation seen by TUKO.co.ke, the only survivors in the crash were the driver of the truck and his turn-boy who are fighting for their lives at Nakuru Level Five Hospital.

"All the 11 passengers and the driver of the shuttle died on the spot," Rongai OCPD Japheth Kioko was quoted as having said by the Daily Nation.

The bodies of the deceased passengers and driver were taken to Nakuru Level Five Hospital mortuary as police make efforts to communicate with their relatives.

Salgaa has been marked as a black spot and many lives have been lost there through road accidents.
