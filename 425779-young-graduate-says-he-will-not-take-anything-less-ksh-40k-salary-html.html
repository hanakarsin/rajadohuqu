Young Graduate Says He Will Not Take Anything Less than KSh 40k as Salary

Publish date: 2024-02-28
  • A young graduate recently revealed that, although he was unemployed, he would reject any job offer with a salary lower than KSh 40,000
  • The confident man who appeared to know what his value is said, he possesses the right skills and certifications to receive a much better salary
  • He also mentioned that receiving anything below KSh 40,000 as salary would be pointless given the high standard of living

A young Ghanaian man recently granted an interview with Citi Newsroom revealed that although he has been jobless for a year now, taking anything less than KSh 40,000 as salary is out of the question.

The bold young man, whose name was not disclosed, made this statement at the just ended Youth Employment Agency (YEA) job fair held at the Accra international conference centre from Thursday, September 9, 2021, to Friday, September 10, 2021.

According to the man, he will reject a monthly salary lower than KSh 40,000 because he has an Information Technology (IT) background, and with his skills and certifications, he expects better pay.

Another reason he brought up is that he lives in Accra, and the cost of living is very high; hence to be paid below KSh 40,000 would be of no help.

Amos Kimutai, 26, a Petroleum Chemistry graduate from Kisii University; Peter Kipng'etich Langat, 24, a diploma in Health Records holder; John Korir, 31, a diploma in Mechanical Engineering holder; and Duncan Korir, 25, who holds a diploma in Electrical Engineering, toil in the quarry for KSh 100 per day.

The frustrated youngsters whose dreams were dimmed by unemployment narrated that they had to work in the quarry to keep their heads above the water after failing to secure formal employment.

On his part, Kimutai said after sending one too many job applications with no success, he turned to crush stones in the quarry for survival.
