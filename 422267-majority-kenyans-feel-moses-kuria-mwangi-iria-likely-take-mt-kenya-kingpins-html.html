Majority of Kenyans Feel Moses Kuria and Mwangi Wa Iria Likely to Take over as Mt Kenya Kingpins

Publish date: 2024-05-19
  • The majority of respondents argued that the two politicians stand out among their Mt Kenya colleagues
  • Those who vouched for Kuria said the lawmaker always playing the role of a clown but has a brilliant mind on finance and economics
  • Other politicians who featured in the list included Speaker Justin Muturi, Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagua, and Governor Anne Waiguru

A section of Kenyans has picked politicians Gatundu South legislator Moses Kuria, and Murang'a governor Mwangi was Iria as potential Mt Kenya kingpins.

According to an online poll conducted by TUKO.co.ke on Thursday, August 12, the majority of respondents argued that the two politicians stand out among their Mt Kenya colleagues.

Those who vouched for Kuria said the lawmaker always playing the role of a clown but has a brilliant mind on finance and economics and prefers to keep his cards close to his chest.

Wa Iria scored highly on his development track record as Murang'a county boss, where he initiated various projects aimed at improving agricultural productivity in the region.

Other politicians who were featured in the list included Speaker Justin Muturi, Mathira MP Rigathi Gachagua, Governor Anne Waiguru, Kimani Ichungwah and Peter Kenneth.

Meanwhile, calls for a political party that will advance the agenda of the Mt Kenya region gained momentum on Thursday, August 12, after Narc Kenya leader Martha Karua, Mwangi Kiunjuri (The Service Party) and Moses Kuria (Chama Cha Kazi) held a meeting.

The trio said they were out to market the parties to other like-minded Kenyans.

Addressing the press at Serena Hotel in Nairobi, Kiunjuri said they had started unifying one by one.

''As TSP, we are very clear; the way forward is that TSP will be on the ballot, the same way NARC-Kenya will be there and Chama Cha Kazi. That one, there is no question about it. It is not whether we are hesitating to join any other political dispensation or we are adamant; the decision is made that TSP will be there in 2022,'' Kiunjuri said.

Kuria used the opportunity to laud Kuria for maintaining the NARC-Kenya party, saying that had not stopped her from engaging other parties.

''We are talking about 16 years. But, that has not stopped her from engaging with other leaders. We want to build political parties as institutions of posterity. We want to have parties that can outlive us as leaders. Our primary mission here is to look at the interests of our region because every leader comes from a particular region,'' Kuria said.

Source: TUKO.co.ke
