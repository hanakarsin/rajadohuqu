Lucy Gichuhi: Kenyan-Born Ex-Australian Senator's Family Fails to Raise KSh 3M to Bury Daughter in K

The family of Australia-based Kenyan-born politician Lucy Gichuhi and her husband William Gichuhi will be forced to bury their first-born daughter Peris Wanjiru in Australia.

The information posted on an online fundraising platform, GoFund, indicated the 33-year-old Wanjiru died in Adelaide, Australia on Thursday, August 26.

The family created a GoFund account to raise KSh 3.3 million towards hospital bills, mortuary as well as air travel to bring the body of the deceased woman back to Kenya for a nice send-off.

“Due to the current COVID-19 restrictions in place that have resulted in decreased air travel to and out of Australia, the cost of this process is overwhelming to the family and thus the appeal to you for your kind support,” it reads.

However, the family was able to raise KSh 44,000 prompting a change in plan.

"This fundraiser is no longer accepting donations. If you are the organiser, beneficiary, a team member or donor, sign in for further details," a statement on the GoFund account read.

The change in the plan means Gichuhi's family will not bring home their lovely late daughter.

An obituary as seen by TUKO.co.ke on Tuesday, September 21, showed there will be a church service at Influencers (Paradise) Church.

The service will then be followed by burial at the Centennial Park Adelaide, South Australia on September 27.

"It is with deep sorrow and humble acceptance of God’s will that we announce the sudden demise of Peris Wanjiru Gichuhi of Adelaide, Australia.....For more than 33 years ago God gave us Peris and now God has taken Peris. We loved Peris but God loved her more," the eulogy read.

The Kenyan woman moved to South Australia with her family in 1999. It is South Australia where she was elected the senator.

She recently published a book, Behind the Smiles in which she recounted and told of her abusive marriage.

Having claimed in the book she had been in a three-decade abusive marriage, her husband, later on, came out to dismiss the claims he abused her in any way.
