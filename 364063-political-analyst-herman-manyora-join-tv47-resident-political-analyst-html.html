Political analyst Herman Manyora to join TV47 as resident political analyst

Publish date: 2024-04-10

- Herman Manyora finalised the agreement with management of TV47 and the show will soon come to your screens hosted by Abubakar Abdullahi

- Manyora has over the past few years established himself as a tenacious political and current affairs analyst

- The show envisions setting the stage for a political agenda in the country ahead of the 2022 General Election

Political analyst Herman Manyora has signed a deal to be TV47’s resident analyst on an upcoming political show to be hosted by news anchor Abubakar Abdullahi.

TUKO.co.ke understands the university don shall co-host the show alongside Abubakar, who is doubling up as the station’s boss following the sacking of CEO Eugene Anangwe.

Over the past few years, Manyora, a PhD student, has established himself as a tenacious political and current affairs analyst.

As the country gears up for major political realignments ahead of the 2022 General Election, TV47 hopes to stage a political show that will set political agenda in the country.

“The one-hour show is expected to be the premier political talk show on the station. It will also host other high profile analysts and guests,” a TV47 insider told TUKO.co.ke.

Political and current affairs shows are popular with Kenyan TV audiences, particularly given how political conversations dominate social interactions.

Name of the show is yet to be agreed upon but it is understood Manyora has finalised the agreement with the management of the media house associated with Mount Kenya University founder, Simon Gicharu.

The Cape Media-owned station was officially launched and rebranded on Friday, February 14, 2020, following a successful six months trial of its content approach and human resource.

It leverages on telling both insightful and entertaining untold stories and has a network of highly experienced reporters across the 47 counties.
