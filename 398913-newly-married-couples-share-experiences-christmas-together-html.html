Newly married couples share their experiences of first Christmas together

Publish date: 2024-04-07

- Whether to spend Christmas in their house, with relatives, or on a getaway can be a difficult decision for newlyweds

- The bone of contention is usually whether the two will go to the husband's family together, or the wife will spend with her people

- Some newly married couples shared their stories on how they navigated the day, with both achievements and failures

Generally, Christmas is a time when people take time off the busy year to eat, drink and spend the holiday with family members.

In most instances, it is usually people catching up on time lost between them and relatives, whom they hardly meet due to the hustles and bustles of the year.

Things, however, become a bit different when two people get into a marriage and create a new setup of two families.

For most men, taking wife home to family is the preferred option, while newly married brides wish they spent it with the folks they were detached from.

A compilation by mywedding.co.ug highlights a number of couples who, although difficult, found a middle ground that enabled them navigate their first Christmas together easily.

Ronald and Grace

When Ronald and Grace got married in 2018, they hosted both families together for Christmas lunch which was followed by exchanging gifts.

The first headache came when they struggled to find an acceptable menu that would cater for both sides when it came to meals.

Grace, also left the event disappointed as her husband’s choice of gift fell short of what she expected.

Two years later, she is also yet to know what her husband thought of the gift she offered.

Lawrence and Joseline

Lawrence and Joseline chose a different path to celebrate the day by doing what Lawrence calls “road trips with my better half.”

Months into their marriage in 2014, the two decided that Christmas was the best day for them to go sightseeing, experiencing new places and getting to know each other better.

Their journey started at Bambarara, snaked through Bwera and Kasese before coming to an end at Fort Portal.

“At the end of the trip we drove back to Namugongo. It was a memorable Christmas,” Lawrence says.

Emma and Margaret

After being declared husband and wife in August 13, 2016, Christmas found Emma and Margaret Awagwaomuwa with a four-month old pregnancy.

Being a hotelier, Emma focused on throwing a memorable Christmas festivity that would bring together friends and relatives.

“I also wanted to show my wife at her best, so we went shopping for an outfit that complimented her four-months pregnant body,” quips Emma.

He says that his newly wedded wife defied the pregnancy and went into festivity mode that impressed both him and his family.

“It was one of the best Christmases of my life. I was also blessed to have enough resources to share with my new in-laws and my parents comfortably,” he concludes.

Timothy and Aidah

Timothy and Aidah Wandabwa celebrated their first Christmas at her home as part of honoring her Kinyankole culture in a tradition called Okutasya Ekyihara.

The essence of the ceremony is to show the official transition of their daughter from a spinster into a married woman.

The event involves uncovering the woman so that relatives can confirm she is still as intact as she was when the family gave her to the new family.
