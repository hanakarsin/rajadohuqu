Paul Kobia: Controversial businessman arrested in connection to fake gold scam

Publish date: 2024-04-11

- Kobia was arrested by detectives from the Special Crimes Unit who raided his residence on Wednesday, June 26

- Also arrested during the raid were 14 other suspects linked to a fake gold syndicate allegedly headed by the controversial businessman

- The DCI noted the arrests were made following a complaint filed from Dubai against Kobia

-The latest arrests come barely a month after a related multi-million fake gold scandal was exposed implicating high-profile individuals

Controversial businessman Paul Kobia has been arrested alongside 14 other suspects in connection with a multi-million fake gold scam.

Kobia was picked up on Wednesday, June 26, by detectives from the Special Crimes Unit who raided his residence in Nairobi after the Directorate of Criminal Investigations (DCI) received complaint from Dubai implicating the businessman in a gold scam.

Confirming the arrests, the DCI noted the businessman had allegedly conned a Dubai-based victim more than KSh 170 million (US$ 1.7 million), of which KSh 14 million (US$ 140,000) was paid in Kenya and the balance settled in Dubai for sale of suspected fake gold.

Upon search, the following items were recovered; seven suspected gold nuggets, six motor vehicles, wooden Central Bank of Kenya (CBK) Thermo Scientific Arton analyser, Platinum Thermo Couple Machine, money counting machine, ETR machine, assorted seals/stamps, and suspected counterfeit goods," the DCI said in a statement.

The other suspects arrested together with Kobia were identified as Simeon Wainaina, Paul Gichuhi, Benjamin Mutisya, Patrick Mweu, Samson Kibet and Faith Kioko.

At the same time, Joyce Wenani, Miriam Nyambura, Consolata Thirindi, James Masai, Doreen Kathambi, Tanya Yvonne Goes and Gabriel Ndururi Murage were also nabbed.

All the suspects are expected to appear in court on Thursday, June 27.

The arrests come barely a month since a related fake gold scandal that shook the nation exploded and landed in trouble some very high-profile Kenyan leaders and state officials whose names were dragged into the messy business transaction.

In the previous gold scandal, the authorities arrested several suspects among them Jared Otieno alias Gerald Otieno who was nabbed during a raid conducted on the dawn on May 16, 2019, at his palatial home in Karen, Nairobi.

Otieno's capture came following arrest of at least eight other suspects including one Congolese, two Tanzanians, one Nigerians and four Kenyans.

The suspects were found in possession of millions of suspected fake US dollars and 100 kilograms of counterfeit gold nuggets.

There were allegations Bungoma senator and Ford Kenya party leader Moses Wetangula was one of the masterminds of the gold scam which saw foreign investors from the United Arab Emirates (UAE) lose over KSh 250 million.

The DCI boss George Kinoti had confirmed they were investigating the allegations implicating the vocal senator in the fraudulent gold business.
