Gideon Moi in Kajiado to thank MCAs for overwhelmingly passing BBI

Publish date: 2024-03-25

- Gideon Moi was hosted by Kajiado governor Joseph Ole Lenku at Amboseli where he met county assembly leadership.

- The senator said elders remain critical to growth of the country and should therefore be consulted to build a resilient society

- He urged the grassroots leaders to rally behind the Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) bill which proposed a raft of constitutional changes

Baringo senator and KANU chairperson Gideon Moi has convened a consultative meeting with Maasai grassroots leaders and opinion shapers to bolster his presidential bid.

Moi who was hosted by Kajiado governor Joseph Ole Lenku at Amboseli, said remain critical to growth of the country and should therefore be consulted to build a resilient society.

The senator whose home county of Baringo is the only one so far to reject Building Bridges Initiative (BBI) bill, kept a brave face urging his audience to rally behind it.

He also used the opportunity to thank Kajiado county assembly adopted the Constitutional Amendment Bill, 2020 after a three-hour debate on Wednesday, February 17.

"While meeting Kajiado MCAs, I expressed my gratitude to them for passing the BBI Constitutional Amendment Bill in their county assembly," he said.

Present during the meeting was the Majority Leader Moipaai Teto, Minority Leader Daniel Naikuni, Majority Whip James Waichangura and Minority Whip Dickson Nkaloyo.

In the debate session that took three hours, 32 Kajiado MCAs overwhelmingly voted in support of the BBI bill while only nine opposed it.
