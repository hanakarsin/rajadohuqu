120 teachers being investigated for swapping banks to avoid repaying loans

Publish date: 2024-03-01

- The teachers are being probed by the Ugandan authorities for planning to default on the salary loans they acquired

- Their union has, however, intervened to negotiate on their behalf with the affected banks to seek an out-of-court resolution

- They were reportedly assisted by top education officers in changing the banks

At least 120 teachers in Uganda have been subjected to probe after they allegedly swapped banks to escape repaying salary loans they acquired.

The teachers from Ntungamo District which is found in the Western part of the country are said to have colluded with top education officials in the district to defraud Centenary and Stanbic, among other banks.

The Uganda National Teachers Union (UNATU) has, however, stepped in to settle the issue on their behalf.

"After getting the loans, they (teachers) were allegedly helped by some people here (at the district headquarters) and reopened their salary accounts in other banks leaving the primary banks without where to cut loan servicing money,” Gideon Kabangizi, UNATU chairperson.
