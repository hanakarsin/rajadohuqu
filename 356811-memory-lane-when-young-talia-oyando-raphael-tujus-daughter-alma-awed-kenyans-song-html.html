Memory lane: When young Talia Oyando, Raphael Tuju's daughter Alma awed Kenyans with their song

Publish date: 2024-02-29

- Talia and Tuju's daughter Alma formed a group called Talma in 2001

- They were so good that they were contracted by Channel O to compose the song Say Yes For Children in the same year

- Talia and her band mate are now successful people both doing good in their different fields

Everyone has a past, be it rosy or messy, we all have moments we live to remember all our lives.

Celebrities just like other people have their pasts too, and celebrated radio girl Talia Oyando is not an exception.

TUKO.co.ke understands the mother of one has done literally everything that falls in the entertainment industry.

She has been a TV presenter, radio host, events emcee and a host of many other colourful gigs in the larger showbiz space.

One thing that most people do not know about her is the fact that she is also a musician, yes, she is.

TUKO.co.ke has learnt Talia, or the Night Nurse as she is popular among her fans, once had a shining music career and even brags of several songs under her belt.

Talia, together with Jubilee Party Secretary General Raphael Tuju's daughter Alma Midayi formed a group called Talma in 2001 and kicked off their singing careers.

They were good enough to be contracted by Channel O to compose the song Say Yes For Children in the same year which became a massive hit.

Many 90s kids can remember the tune and quite a number, through social media posts and comments, admitted indeed Talia is a ''legend'', having been around for ages.

For those in the dark, the lass' parents played a big role in introducing her to the entertainment industry at a very young age.

Unlike most conventional 19th century parents, Talia's supported her through her journey in the industry.

As for her band mate Alma, she is now married to Grado Victor Mabachi.

She is also the managing director at Entim Sidai Wellness Sanctuary while her hubby is a lawyer by profession and partner at Waruhiu and Co Advocates.
