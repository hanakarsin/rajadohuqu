A mother who delivered a baby girl while battling COVID-19 is honouring her Intensive Care Unit (ICU) nurse by naming her the child's godmother.

Monique Jones was 26 weeks pregnant with her second child when she was admitted to Barnes-Jewish Hospital in St. Louis, Missouri on September 4, 2020, and put in the ICU with severe COVID-19 symptoms.

Two days later, the 28-year-old was intubated and placed on an extracorporeal membrane oxygenation machine, CNN reported.

The ECMO machine often called the "highest form of life support," uses a pump to circulate a patient's blood through an artificial lung.

Jones said she was nervous and scared for the procedure. She was cared for by ICU nurse Caitlyn Obrock when she was sedated.

On each shift, the nurse said she would whisper words of encouragement to Jones to let her know she was going to make it through.

"With Monique being pregnant, she's only a year older than I am, I just felt, like, a strong connection with wanting her to do well," said Obrock.

"She truthfully was a light to me. You know, we have to hold out hope," she added.

The new mom said that as she battled her disease, she could sense the positive energy around her.

When her health deteriorated to the point that the medical team was not sure if she was going to do it without delivering the infant, Obrock said they decided to deliver her through an emergency cesarean section.

Jones' daughter Zamyrah was born at 29 weeks of age on September 23, weighing just 2 pounds and 5 ounces.

The baby was moved to St. Louis Children's Hospital after birth, where she was placed in the neonatal ICU.

Jones stayed at Barnes-Jewish Hospital, and Obrock put Zamyrah's photographs in Jones's room.

After the birth of her daughter, her condition started to improve, Obrock said.

She was removed from the ECMO machine on October 1 and moved to a rehabilitation hospital a few days later, where she had to learn to walk again.

On October 20, nearly a month after giving birth, she first carried Zamyrah.

Obrock arranged a baby shower for the young mother with presents for the newborn and $2,000 (KSh 218,700) for Jones and her family while Jones was in recovery.

Zamyrah returned home on December 10, from the hospital and continues to do well, Jones said.

The bond between Obrock and Jones continued to grow with the saying they texted every day.

As a way of thanking her for all her support, Jones agreed to make Obrock a godmother to Zamyrah.

"A godmother to me is like a support system. And I felt like she fought for me," Jones said.

Jones says she's still recovering from her illness and has set up a GoFundMe for her medical bills to raise funds.
