How to become a pharmacist in Kenya: Requirements and institutions

Publish date: 0001-01-01

Students who excel in their K.C.S.E pick pharmacy as a career path because of its potential rewards after medical school. Additionally, this course exposes them to a whole new world of science emphasising medical drug administration. However, there are minimum requirements that you must meet for you to become a pharmacist in Kenya.

Pharmacy plays a significant role in the country's health sector. Pharmacists are responsible for prescribing and dispensing medication, disease prevention and management, and patient counselling in hospitals, institutions, or community outlets. So, how can you become a pharmacist in Kenya?

How to become a pharmacist in Kenya

To be registered as a pharmacist in Kenya, you must be a holder of a Bachelor's degree in Pharmacy for four years, followed by a one-year supervised internship regulated by the Pharmacy and Poisons Board.

Students who undertake the degree program abroad have to sit for a prior internship examination. It entails six months of hospital pharmacy-based practice, three months in a community (retail) pharmacy, and three months practicum in the pharmaceutical industry. Upon completion of their internship, practitioners undertake a registration examination administered by the Pharmacy and Poisons Board.

Upon completing the course, pharmacists should participate in continuing professional development activities to stay up-to-date with the latest pharmaceutical development. The Pharmaceutical Society of Kenya (PSK) offers these programs.

PSK is the professional body that promotes common standards for professional conduct and a code of ethics. The body also advocates for the welfare of pharmacists.

Where to study pharmacy in Kenya

Several netizens have been asking, ''Is pharmacy marketable in Kenya?'' Yes, the course is one of the most marketable in the country. The numbers have significantly improved since the first Kenya-trained pharmacists graduated in 1978 from a single public university.

By 2013, there were about 3,500 pharmacists in the country, and the number has likely increased. However, these professionals still have numerous opportunities to work in public and private hospitals, community pharmacies, administrative positions, and hospital-based research.

Today, eight universities in the country offer a degree in pharmacy. The Commission of University Education and the Pharmacy and Poisons Board must approve these private and public schools. Here are the institutions where you can pursue a pharmacy course:

1. The University of Nairobi

The five-year course runs for 36 weeks annually. Regular students pay approximately KShs 475,000. The requirements to pursue this course are:

  • Mean grade of C+ and above in K.C.S.E
  • Minimum of C+ in the cluster subjects, including Biology, Chemistry, Physics or Mathematics, and English or Kiswahili.

2. Mount Kenya University

Mount Kenya University is another institution that offers pharmacy courses in Kenya. The school was the first private institution to provide the pharmacy degree program in Kenya. The tuition fee is at least Ksh 156,825 per semester. To gain admission to the institution, applicants need to meet the following requirements:

  • Mean Grade of B- and above
  • C+ in Mathematics/Physics, Chemistry/Physics? Biology?Biological Science and English/Kiswahili.
  • A-Level candidates should have a minimum of two entry principal passes in Biology and Chemistry and a subsidiary pass in Mathematics/Physics.

3. Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology

The Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology is a public institution situated in Juja. The pharmacy program started in 2011. Interested candidates should have a KCSE mean grade of B- with C+ in Biology/Biological Sciences, Chemistry, Mathematics/Physics, and English/Kiswahili.

4. Kenya Methodist University

The Kenya Methodist University, popularly known as KEMU, offers a five-year pharmacy program on the main campus. Government-sponsored students approximately KShs 27,500 per semester. The course requirements are as follows:

  • KCSE mean grade of B-
  • Equivalent qualification of C+ in Chemistry/Biology, Maths/Physics, English/Kiswahili and cluster B; Biological Sciences/Physiological Sciences, Mathematics, English/Kiswahili.

5. Kenyatta University

Kenyatta University is a public institution that also offers a degree in pharmacy. The school established the department in 2018. The school fee ranges between KShs 400,000 to KShs 515,000. The minimum requirements include:

  • Mean grade of C+
  • Cluster weight of B- in Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics/Physical Sciences, and English/Kiswahili.
  • The second alternative is a cluster weight of B- in Biological Sciences, Physical Sciences, Mathematics, and English/Kiswahili.

6. Kabarak University

Kabarak University offers the program on its main campus. Interested candidates must meet the following criteria:

  • Mean Grade of B- in K.C.S.E
  • C+ and above in the following cluster subjects: Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics/Physics, English/Kiswahili, Biological Sciences, Physical Sciences, Mathematics, English/Kiswahili.

7. United States International University (USIU)

USIU also offers pharmacy courses for candidates wishing to pursue a career in the field. Applicants must have an average weight of B- and no cluster subject shall have a grade below C+. These subjects include Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics/Physics and English/Kiswahili, or Biological Sciences, Physical Sciences, Mathematics, English/Kiswahili.

8. Kisii University

Kisii University also offers a pharmacy degree on its main campus. The course runs for 3-4 years. In addition, the institution offers several e-learning programs. However, it is unclear whether learners can pursue online pharmacy in Kenya at the university.

Diploma in pharmacy

If you do not meet the requirements for the Bachelor's degree, you don't have to worry. Numerous middle-level colleges offer the pharmacy diploma program, an entry-level credential offered to many undergraduate candidates.

The course duration is three years, after which the students are registered by the Pharmacy and Poisons Board as pharmaceutical technologists, popularly known as pharm techs. The diploma in pharmacy requirements is a KCSE Mean Grade of C plain, with a C plain in Chemistry, Biology, Mathematics/Physics, and English/Kiswahili.

Once you complete the diploma program, you can enrol in the undergraduate program in any recognized institution. In addition, most diploma students are exempted from some courses at the degree level.

Pharmacist salary in Kenya

According to Glassdoor, a pharmacist in Kenya takes home an average of KShs 140,000 monthly. The average base pay for these professionals is KShs 120,000. However, pharmacists' salary depends on the contract they sign with the employer. Other factors to consider include years of experience and educational background.

On the other hand, pharm techs earn an entry-level base salary of KShs 35,000. Mid-level pharm techs who have worked for three to nine years take home an average of KShs 45,000. Senior-level pharmaceutical technologists earn an average of KShs 53,000. However, their pay also varies with the contract they sign with their employer.
