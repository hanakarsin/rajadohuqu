How to renew a Certificate of Good Conduct in Kenya: Full guide

Publish date: 2024-07-14

Having a certificate of good conduct is very crucial especially to job seekers. Thanks to the many advancements being made by the government on the public service delivery platforms, application for certificate of good conduct is now very easy. Most of the government offices now offer more organized and fast services. Gone are the days when you would look for a middleman/ facilitator to help in processing your application for certificate of good conduct.

The application process of the certificate of good conduct Kenya must be done in person, whether you’re a Kenyan citizen or a foreigner. This is because when applying for a certificate of good conduct in Kenya, your fingerprint data has to be collected. The physical application for the certificate of good conduct can be done at any police station (though will take longer to process roughly 2 to 3 weeks), the CID Headquarters/ Directorate of Criminal Investigations (takes one week to process) or at the Huduma Centers.

How to renew a certificate of good conduct

Even though there is the certificate of good conduct Kenya E-citizen option (certificate of good conduct Kenya online), you will still have to make a physical appearance at the CID headquarters. This is because the process involves taking your fingerprints.

Important documents to have when applying for your Certificate of Good Conduct in Kenya

When submitting your application for a certificate of good conduct in Kenya, you will need to have the documents below. The documents are different depending on whether you are a Kenyan citizen or a foreigner.

For the application for certificate of good conduct Kenya E-citizen applicants, downloaded and printed copies of the C24 (both sides) and the invoice (2 copies). These documents should be printed on A4 papers. Remember application for certificate of conduct online option is only available for Kenyan Citizens, foreigners have to apply at the CID headquarters.

Documents required for Kenyan citizens

  • Original national ID and a copy.
  • Original birth certificate for applicants who are under 18 years.
  • Ksh. 1,000 processing fee.

Documents required for foreigners

  • Original passport of their country and a copy of the passport.
  • Proof of residency in Kenya for more than six months.
  • Ksh. 1,000 processing fee.
  • Dependent’s passes.
  • For expatriates, documents from employers or institutions will be required.
  • Valid work permits.
  • For students, pupil passes/student IDs will be needed together with supporting relevant testimonials or certificates showing completion of the academic course.

How to make an application for a certificate of good conduct

There are only two options available for Kenyan citizens when it comes to submitting an application for certificate of good conduct in Kenya. As already mentioned, physical application of certificate of good conduct is made at the CID Headquarters, Huduma Centers or nearby police station and then the certificate of good conduct Kenya online application which will require a physical submission. Here are the processes of application for certificate of good conduct.

Physical application for certificate of good conduct

The application process for certificate of good conduct in Kenya is straightforward and fast. Carry all the required documents (both original and photocopies). You should also carry the processing fee. At the application station of your choice, you will be guided by an official on the steps to follow. With all the required documents, you should be done with the application for certificate of good conduct in a few minutes.

Note: Physical application of certificate of good conduct is currently not available. You have to apply online, book appointment, go for finger print taking and download your E-police clearance certificate.

Certificate of Good Conduct Kenya online application

The certificate of good conduct Kenya online application is made via the E-citizen platform. Payment is made via credit, debit cards or mobile money (remember to print the invoice as proof of payment). Start by reading the instructions and then fill out the certificate of good conduct E-citizen application form. You will then select your preferred mode of payment and then make your payments. Finally, download and print the invoice (2 copies) and the C24 (printed on both sides of one A4 paper).

You will then present yourself and the required documents to the CID headquarters for the finger print collection process. Parents can submit the application documents for the certificate of good conduct Kenya online on behalf of their underage children. The certificate of good conduct Kenya E-citizen payment costs Ksh. 1050.

Picking your certificate of Good Conduct in Kenya

You will not be able to get your certificate of good conduct, the same day you submit the application for certificate of good conduct. There is a waiting period that varies depending on your venue of application. At the end of the application for certificate of good conduct, you will be given a receipt that you will submit with your original national ID when picking up your certificate of good conduct. The receipt will have a pickup date, mostly ranging from one to three weeks.
