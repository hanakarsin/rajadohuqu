Tougher times ahead as Kenya Power seeks to increase cost of electricity by 20 percent

Publish date: 2024-03-28

- Kenya Power seeks to increase charges for consumers using less than 100 kilowatts per month from KSh 10 to KSh 12.50 per unit

- Households consuming 200 units on the other hand will have their cost of power increased by 18.8% from KSh 4,612 to KSh 5,477

- Energy Regulatory Authority said it was reviewing KPLC's application

- The move by the listed utility firm comes after it suspended afternoon tea for its staff

Kenyans will have to dig deeper into their pockets to pay for power after Kenya Power made an application to increase the cost of electricity by 20%.

The company has sought approval from the Energy and Petroleum Regulatory Authority (EPRA) to increase charges for consumers using less than 100 kilowatts per month from KSh 10 to KSh 12.50 per unit.

The move by the listed utility firm will see the cost of electricity for households consuming 50 units a month increase by 17.85% from KSh 816 to KSh 961.

Homes consuming 200 units on the other hand will have their cost of power increased by 18.8% from KSh 4,612 to KSh 5,477 if the regulator approves Kenya Power’s new proposal to review tariff rates.

Energy Regulatory and Petroleum Authority Director General Paivel Oimeke said on Monday, November 25, the authority was reviewing Kenya Power’s application saying all stakeholders will be involved before a final decision is made.

“The tariff application is still under review by EPRA and stakeholder consultations will be held before any determination is made,” Oimeke was quoted by Business Daily.

The company’s latest move has angered a section of Kenyans on social media.

The move by KPLC to increase tariffs comes one week after it suspended its afternoon tea for staff to cut costs.

The firm had earlier issued a profit earning for the 2018/19 financial year saying its profit were expected to decline by 25%.
