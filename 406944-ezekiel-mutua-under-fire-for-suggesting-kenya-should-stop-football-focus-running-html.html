Ezekiel Mutua Under Fire for Suggesting Kenya Should Drop Football, Focus on Running

Publish date: 2024-05-27

- Kenya Film and Classification Board (KFCB) CEO Ezekiel Mutua sneered at the national team after the 1 - 1 draw with Egypt

- He advised that Kenya should focus on running instead of football because the former had proved to be worthy

- Netizens, however, felt the Harambee Stars team deserved accolades for the unforeseen performance given they were facing a star-studded Egyptian side

Netizens have unleashed their wrath on the Kenya Film and Classification Board (KFCB) CEO Ezekiel Mutua over a statement he sent via a tweet and which many found to be offensive.

Mutua took to Twitter to express his frustrations after Kenya on the night of Thursday, March 25, failed to qualify for the next stage of the AFCON challenge after Harambee Stars held Egypt to a 1-1 draw at Kasarani Stadium.

He suggested that Kenya should focus on other viable sports like athletics instead of soccer which, according to him, has not been bringing forth results.

"Kenya 1 - 1 Egypt. Why do we force ourselves with soccer? Why can't we just stick to running!" he posed.

It is the above statement that rubbed the netizens the wrong way. They felt that Mutua, because of his status, should have cheered the players up rather than discourage them given the 1- 1 draw was a stellar performance.

Harambee Stars were taking on a star-studded Egypt which had been given an upper hand in the Thursday clash. Therefore the draw was akin to a win and netizens felt Mutua should have joined other leaders in cheering the Kenyan side.

Despite the spirited fight put up by Harambee Stars on Thursday, the team will not be making an appearance at the AFCON finals slated for next year in Cameroon.

Stars were desperate for a win during the Group G clash played at Moi Sports Centre Kasarani but unfortunately, they failed at home.

Egypt took an early lead following Afsha's goal just two minutes into the kick-off.

However, Kenya through Abdallah Hassan managed to turn the tables in the 65th minute thus restoring parity after slotting home from close range.

The home side's hopes of springing a comeback were dashed 15 minutes to time after they were reduced to 10 men following full-back Johnstone Omurwa's red-card for elbowing Mostafa Mohamed.

The two teams eventually settled for a point each, with Egypt and Comoros booking a place in the Afcon finals with a game to spare.

Stars will be taking on Togo in Lome in their last group match, the clash being inconsequential.
