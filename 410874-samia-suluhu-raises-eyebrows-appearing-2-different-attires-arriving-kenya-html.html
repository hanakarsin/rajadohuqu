Samia Suluhu Raises Eyebrows after Appearing in 2 Different Attires after Arriving in Kenya

Publish date: 2024-08-21

- President Samia Suluhu made her maiden visit to Kenya on Wednesday, May 4

- She jetted into the country during the morning hours of the day before making her way to State House, Nairobi where President Uhuru Kenyatta received her

- Her change of attires during the first day of the visit is what caught the attention of many who could not hide their bafflement

Tanzania's President Samia Suluhu has left many talking after appearing in two different attires while on the first day of her two-day state visit to Kenya.

While touching down at the Jomo Kenyatta International Airport (JKIA) on Tuesday morning, the Tanzanian leader donned a brown suit crowned by a dark brown hijab.

Later at the Nairobi State House with her host, President Uhuru Kenyatta, Samia matched a maroon outfit, completing her look with a purple hijab.

This left netizens puzzling at the never-seen-before order that seemed to be special given the little time span within which it happened.

Going by an observation by one of the tweeps who seemed quite informed, the relevant security protocols occasioned the unforeseen change of attires.

"In line with KDF traditions, Tanzania President Samia Suluhu had to change her clothes because they resembled that of KDF stand-in aide-de-camp. Once a president visits a foreign country, security details are taken by the host country," noted Edith Wanyama.

Samia's visit happens to be her second out of Tanzania since March 18, when she was sworn in as president.

The 61-year-old visited Uganda a fortnight ago, marking her first visit outside Tanzania as the nation's president.

On April 10, President Uhuru Kenyatta sent Sports Cabinet Secretary Amina Mohamed to hold diplomatic talks with Suluhu in what was viewed as one strategy to solve the disputes between Dodoma and Nairobi.

During the visit, Uhuru, through Amina, extended an olive branch to Suluhu to mend the frosty relations between the two East African countries.

In the course of her visit to Kenya, the first woman president in the East African region is slated to address a joint sitting of parliament, the event taking place on Wednesday, May 5.
