Ken Okoth: Raila Odinga, Kalonzo Musyoka lead politicians in mourning Kibra MP

Publish date: 0001-01-01

Leaders drawn from the political and corporate spheres have described demise of Kibra MP Ken Okoth as a big blow not only to Kenyans but Africa as a whole.

Led by Opposition politicians Raila Odinga and Kalonzo Musyoka, the leaders expressed their shock at the untimely death of the man many described as a brave champion of equal education.

The Starehe Boys Centre alumnus has been fighting cancer for close to six months and was in and out of hospital both locally and overseas.

Okoth was diagnosed with stage four colorectal cancer which he earlier indicated had brought a complete change in his life.

The 41-year-old born and bred in Kibra slum, returned to Kenya in July 2019 from France where he had done to seek medication.

However, despite his illness, the late MP vowed to remain strong and stand tall once he completed chemotherapy drugs he was on.

When news about his health condition first hit headlines, Kenyans from all walks of life poured in encouraging messages and prayed for the MP to get well.
