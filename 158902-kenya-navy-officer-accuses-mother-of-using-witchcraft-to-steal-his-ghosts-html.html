Military accused of using witchcraft to steal officer's ghosts

Publish date: 2024-02-01

- A Kenya Navy officer revealed to the media that he misplaced his ghosts after they were stolen from him 

- Apparently, his mother and the military colluded to use witchcraft to take them away

- The ghosts were used to foretell attacks by criminals and terrorists in Mombasa, a service he was awarded for and well compensated by the government

A Kenya Navy officer accused his mother and four colleagues of colluding to steal his special ghosts from him.

Mohamed Mohamud, 46, told the Standard on Friday, July 22, that the five used witchcraft to trap the ghosts which normally resided in his body.

The military officers denied stealing the ghosts and instead blamed his mother.

The ruhani are common among coastal people and are known to have special powers of foretelling the future.

Mohamud used his ruhani to report security matters to police in Mombasa.

He also updated the military on impending attacks and the whereabouts of dangerous criminals, service that earned him the Secret Security Agents award.

Since the ghosts were stolen, Mohamud has had to endure health problems.

He has since seen doctors at Defence Forces Memorial Hospital where his blood level was found to be low and his heartbeat irregular.

Apparently, if the spirits die, so does Mohamud. He has had them for 27 years.

A witchdoctor who spoke to the Standard explained that the health toll on Mohamud’s body was as a result of the ruhani not being at peace.
