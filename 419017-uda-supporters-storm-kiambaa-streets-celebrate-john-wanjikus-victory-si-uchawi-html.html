UDA Supporters Storm Kiambaa Streets to Celebrate John Wanjiku's Victory: &quot;Si Uchawi&quot;

Publish date: 2024-07-29
  • On Friday, July 16, UDA supporters took to Kiambaa streets to celebrate their victory in Kiambaa by-elections
  • Nominated senator Millicent Omanga also took to her social media platforms to celebrate the victory of John Wanjiku
  • UDA supporters said they won the election through prayer, not witchcraft

Supporters for the United Democratic Alliance (UDA) party in the Kiambaa constituency have taken the streets to celebrate John Wanjiku's victory in the just-ended by-election.

Prayer works

In a video clip seen by TUKO.co.ke, the supporters are seen dancing in the streets chanting that they have won the election through prayer, not witchcraft.

Nominated senator Millicent Omanga also took to her social media account to celebrate the victory of Wanjiku.

"Congratulations Kawanjiku and UDA fraternity, for the well-deserved victory in Kiambaa. Thanks for delivering it to the hustlers. The journey has just begun," she tweeted.

IEBC declares

This comes after the Independent Electoral and Boundaries Commission (IEBC) declared Wanjiku the winner in the hotly contested Kiambaa by-election.

Wanjiku floored his close rival Kariri Njama of the ruling Jubilee Party.

The UDA flagbearer in the by-election garnered 21,773 votes, while Njuguna, his closest opponent, managed to get 21,263 votes. The votes between the two candidates were 500.

Taking to his official Twitter account, Wanjiku, who becomes the first Member of Parliament (MP) from the UDA party, attributed his victory to God.

''God bless Kiambaa, God bless Kenya!'' the tweet reads.

Besides Wanjiku and Njuguna, other candidates in the race were Richard Njoroge of The New Democrats Party, Isaac Karomo( Federal Party of Kenya), Willie Mbugua (Independent), David Mugo (Independent), Evans Wainaina, and Njoroge Harrison (Independent).
